Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Bangladeshi economist and social entrepreneur, is renowned for his pioneering work in microfinance. As the founder of Grameen Bank, he revolutionized lending practices by providing small, unsecured loans to impoverished individuals, particularly women, who were traditionally excluded from traditional banking systems. This innovative approach, often referred to as microcredit, empowered millions to escape poverty, start businesses, and improve their livelihoods. Yunus's groundbreaking work earned him global recognition, culminating in the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006. His contributions have not only alleviated poverty but also inspired social entrepreneurship movements worldwide, demonstrating the transformative power of financial inclusion and social justice.
